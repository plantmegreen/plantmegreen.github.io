Rose of Sharon, scientifically known as Hibiscus syriacus, is a magnificent flowering plant that adds a touch of elegance to any garden. With its vibrant colors and exotic blooms, it resembles a small tree that can reach impressive heights. In this article, we will explore the 17 best Rose of Sharon varieties that you should consider growing in your garden to introduce a burst of color and beauty.

1. Pink: Graceful Pastel Blooms#

One of the most stunning Rose of Sharon varieties is the Pink variety. Its large, pastel pink blooms measure an impressive four to six inches across. The fuzzy foliage adds an intriguing texture to the overall appearance of the plant. Pink Rose of Sharon prefers full sun or partial shade and can grow up to eight to twelve feet tall and six to ten feet wide.

2. Marina: Mesmerizing Blue Stain#

Known as the Blue Stain, Marina Rose of Sharon captivates with its royal blue petals adorned with a small purple center. The light yellow pistol beside the stamen enhances the beauty of the flowers, making them stand out in any garden.

3. Bluebird: Vivid Violet Blue Delight#

Bluebird Rose of Sharon boasts vibrant violet-blue blooms with a stunning purple center. The white stamens create an eye-catching contrast against the intense color of the petals, making this variety a true showstopper in any landscape.

4. Ossio Blue: Elegance in Bloom#

Ossio Blue is an exquisite Rose of Sharon variety with small, velvety petals and a bright purple base at the center of the bloom. The flower heads can grow up to three to four inches across, adding an elegant touch to any garden.

5. Little Kim: A Small Wonder#

If you prefer a more compact Rose of Sharon, the Little Kim variety is perfect for you. It features flowers with white petals and purple patches at the tips, reminiscent of the classic Purple Heart variety. Despite its diminutive size, Little Kim can add a touch of charm to any garden space.

6. Pink Chiffon: A Showstopper in Pastel Blue#

Pink Chiffon is an award-winning semi-double variety with pastel blue flowers. The outer petals are round and broad, while the inner ones are thin, small, and elongated, creating a unique and captivating aesthetic appeal.

7. Aphrodite: Ornamental Beauty in Pink#

Aphrodite Rose of Sharon displays striking ornamental blooms with a rich pink hue and a dark red central patch. When laden with flowers, this variety looks absolutely breathtaking, adding a touch of elegance to any garden setting.

8. Lucy: Double Blooms in Vibrant Magenta#

Lucy Rose of Sharon stands out with its gorgeous double blooms in bright and deep magenta colors. From a distance, these blooms resemble beautiful roses, adding a touch of elegance and sophistication wherever they are planted. This variety thrives in full sun exposure.

9. Purple Heart: Bold Blooms with a Maroon Blush#

Purple Heart is a fascinating Rose of Sharon variety that produces stunning purple blooms with a red maroon blush around the center. This robust plant can reach a height of five to nine feet and a width of four to six feet, creating a majestic presence in any garden.

10. Orchid Satin: Lavender Pink Beauty#

Orchid Satin is a showy Rose of Sharon variety known for its large flower heads with round, broad petals and a captivating red center. This plant graces the garden with lavender pink flowers throughout the summer, providing a delightful visual treat.

11. Minerva: Classic Lavender Magenta Charm#

Minerva Rose of Sharon is a classic variety with bright lavender magenta blossoms that boast striking red centers. The white flower heads, measuring approximately three to five inches wide, add an element of elegance and grace to any garden landscape.

12. Blue Chiffon: Royal Beauty with Purple Petals#

Blue Chiffon is a semi-double Rose of Sharon variety that displays magnificent purple petals. This outstanding variety has received the prestigious Garden Merit award from the Royal Horticulture Society for its exceptionally beautiful flowers.

13. Sugar Tip: Graceful Double Blooms#

Sugar Tip Rose of Sharon offers delicate double blooms in a charming pink shade, complemented by bright variegated cream white and blue-green leaves. What sets this cultivar apart is its inability to set seeds, making it an excellent choice for those who prefer a low-maintenance plant.

14. Blueberry Smoothie: A Delicious Display#

Blueberry Smoothie Rose of Sharon is another visually stunning variety that sports four to five-inch-wide double flowers in bluish-purple hues. Standing tall at six to nine feet, this plant thrives in full sun and blooms abundantly, adding a touch of grandeur to any garden.

15. Lavender Chiffon: A Symphony of Purple Petals#

Native to China and India, Lavender Chiffon Rose of Sharon boasts semi-double blooms with exquisite red veins and delicate purple petals. With a multi-stemmed, rounded growth habit, this variety creates a captivating visual display in any garden space.

16. Red Heart: A Stunning Touch of Red#

Red Heart Rose of Sharon features white petals with a red center that gradually transforms into a deep magenta hue over time. This variety blooms from late summer to fall, adding a touch of vibrant color to the garden during the cooler months.

17. Diana: Pure Elegance in White#

Diana Rose of Sharon epitomizes pure elegance with its mesmerizing white petals, pistols, and stamens. The large flowers measure five to six inches across and stand out beautifully against the plant’s vibrant green foliage.

Now that you have explored the 17 best Rose of Sharon varieties, you might be wondering which one to choose for your garden. Consider the colors, sizes, and overall aesthetics of each variety to find the perfect match for your personal taste and garden setting.
